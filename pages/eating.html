Kyle... the Semi-Picky Eater?

To know who I am as an eater, one must first know my eating habits. I’d consider myself to be a semi-picky eater and a food-texturist (yes i made up this word). I’m not all the way picky, but I definitely have my moments. Most of my food hang ups are pretty context based and conditional. By this I mean that my opinion about a particular food item changes depending on what’s around it. You’re thinking to yourself, that’s pretty normal, but just wait.

Exhibit A: Cheese
Before I start, I should say I have no idea how these things came to be, but I’m convinced that something had to have happened.

I LOVE Mac and Cheese and Quesadillas - and pizza. And while there are some pretty strange combos of cheese that can throw off these dishes for me, I’d say they’re both one of my safe bets. Meaning I’ll eat it anytime, anywhere. However, put ANY kind of cheese on my burger (or sandwhich) and I will almost certainly become nauseous.

When I was younger, I would always ask for no cheese on my hamburgers from McDonald’s, but of course, occasionally, I would unwrap this perfectly made creation only to find cheese - and onions, but more on that later. Many a car ride home from McDonald’s, I would be expertly perform life-preseving surgery for burgers, making sure to act fast so that the cheese wouldn’t melt beyound the point of saving.

Everything that I’ve said, applies to all burgers and sandwiches alike.
To summarize:
Quesadilla and Mac and Cheese: happy face
Burger or Sandwhich with Cheese: sad face

Exhibit B: Onions (& Bell Peppers)
No. Never. Just no thank you. I’ll eat around them.

Okay to be fair I can get through eating some onions, but it’s just that, getting through, my eating experience is enhanced tenfold without the presence of onions…
My reasoning? The texture… no matter what you put onions in they are always noticeable…
And for some reason me, my brain, and tastebuds would rather life without.

That’s all. (Same applies to bell peppers)

Exhibit C: Broccoli & Cabbage

Abolish both of these vegetables right now. I have never liked either since as far back as I can remember. Yet, for some reason, my household was always eating either broccoli or cabbage as the vegetable for dinner…

I'd say I'm justified

Exhibit D: Mushrooms

If Im being honest, mushrooms have really never done any wrong to me, it’s really just their texture… I get squeamish thinking about cooked mushrooms.

Exhibit E: Chocolate

Yes, you read that right. If you read this far, I feel that you deserve to know that I do not in fact like chocolate; though, there are a few exceptions. I CAN eat chocolate as long as its not too chocolatey… For example, I like snickers and most chocolate-caramel combinations but nothing more chocolatey than that. Sometimes I’ll eat brownies and cookies, but I really could live without it…

I’m just more of a french vanilla or vanilla bean gal myself.
Dark chocolate?! NEVER!!
